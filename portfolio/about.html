Hi, I’m Theresa Foley, a senior at the University of Massachusetts Amherst pursuing a dual degree in Computer Science and English, with concentrations in Professional Technical Writing and Creative Writing. My academic journey bridges two worlds—where logic meets imagination, and coding coexists with storytelling. I’m deeply interested in the ethical and human-centered intersections of technology and language, and I aim to build a career that integrates both.

I've contributed to projects ranging from user interface recommendations for an augmented reality 3D printing company (CADSPARC, LLC), to teaching programming to elementary and middle school students through gbSTEM. There, I introduced children to Scratch and Snap, helping them build their own games and interactive animations. I also participated in the Oxford Summer Seminar, where I studied British detective fiction and the work of Jane Austen—an experience that sharpened my literary analysis and deepened my appreciation for storytelling across cultures.

My technical background includes programming languages like Java, C++, Scratch, and Snap. I’ve also worked in various service roles, from dining and customer service to security, which have built my adaptability, teamwork, and empathy. I’m currently exploring web development, ethical computing, and user-centered design.

Creative writing remains a central passion. My poetry and prose often explore themes of revolution, identity, resistance, and the surreal. I challenge traditional form and voice to engage readers in bold, often mystical explorations of the human condition. I’m an active member of campus communities like WiCS (Women in Informatics and Computer Science), the K-Pop Dance Club, and several language learning groups.

Above all, I believe in the transformative power of storytelling, compassion, and interdisciplinary thinking. Whether in technical projects or creative work, I strive to spark conversation, build connection, and inspire positive change.
