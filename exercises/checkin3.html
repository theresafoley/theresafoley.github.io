Poetry & Prose

Delve into a compelling selection of poetry and experimental prose that boldly explores themes of identity, revolution, and the complexities of the human condition. These works challenge conventional form and language, offering powerful reflections on personal and collective transformation, resistance against injustice, and the search for meaning in a rapidly changing world. Whether lyrical, fragmented, or surreal, each piece invites readers to engage deeply with questions of selfhood, society, and the enduring spirit of resilience.

About the Author

Theresa Foley is a senior at the University of Massachusetts Amherst, pursuing a dual degree in Computer Science and English with concentrations in Professional Technical Writing and Creative Writing. Her interdisciplinary academic background bridges analytical precision with literary expression, allowing her to explore innovative intersections between technology, language, and narrative. Theresa has contributed to both technical and creative projects, with experience in ethical computing, user-centered design, and digital publishing. Passionate about literature, social justice, and the transformative power of storytelling, she continues to develop work that reflects a commitment to both craft and critical inquiry.
